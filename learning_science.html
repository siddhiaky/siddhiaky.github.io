WHAT IS HYDROPONICS?

Hydroponics is the science of growing plants in clay pebbles, rocks, and water without using soil. This technique uses nutrients and water to the plant’s roots so that the plant gets its food and nutrition.
Since, plants use a lot of energy growing roots, they can search for the water and nutrients in the soil to survive.
By providing constant available nutrition, hydroponics allow plants to grow up to 50% faster than they do in soil.


PROBLEM


Can we increase nutrient levels in plants? The problem is soil can provide limited nutrients to the plants.


SOLUTION


You can enhance a plant’s nutrient level by simply adding nutrients to the solution they are grown in.
You can also add minerals like calcium, magnesium or any other mineral of your choice.

ABSTRACT


Does every plant perform well in the hydroponic system? To know this answer I carried out an experiment and
planted cilantro in our newly
developed hydroponic system.
To compare their growth we planted them on soil as well. The growth of cilantro was faster in the hydroponic

A plant with roots in soil has to work hard to extract its nutrition from the soil, and it can waste a lot of
energy doing that.
But a plant in nutrient-rich water can spend its energy growing bigger leaves, fruits, and flowers in a shorter
amount of time.


BACKGROUND


Many of the fresh fruits and vegetables you buy in the store are grown hydroponically, especially during the
winter months or in colder climates.
A balanced pH level is important for the healthy growth of a plant. Without a balanced pH, the plant cannot
absorb nutrients easily.
pH value is a scale of 0-14. 7 is neutral, below 7 is acid, above 7 is Alkaline (Base).
Most plants prefer a pH between 5.0 and 7.5.Cilantro will grow in a relatively wide pH range between 6.1 and
7.8.

MATERIALS

  • Clay Pebbles, Rocks, and Soil
  • pH down and pH Up liquid
  • Nutrient liquid and USB cable
  • Planting Cups, Plastic cups
  • 2.5 Gallons of water, 5 Gallons Bucket with lid
  • PVC Pipe, Plastic tubing
  • Water Pump, Mini Sprinkler, Water leveler
  • Male Adapter, Shrub Adapter, and Power Adapter
  • ESP 32 Dev Module board
  • Screwdriver and 4 Screws
  • Cilantro seed (Coriandrum sativum)
  • Board circuit case
  • Coupler
  • Soil and pH meter

EXPERIMENTAL PROCEDURE

  1. First plant the cilantro seeds in a plastic pot.
  2. Make a hole through the PVC pipe and place the plastic tubing through it .
  3. Connect a male adapter to the top of plastic tubing and at the bottom connect it to one end of the coupler.
    Take the other end and connect it with the water pump.
  4. Connect the other end of the male adapter with the shrub adapter. Connect the mini sprinkler on the top of
    the shrub adapter
  5. Make a hole at the bottom of the bucket and place the water leveler.
  6. Go to blynk(an app) and make an account, get your ssid, password, and Auth token(they will email you the
    Auth token).you can buy a button to help you keep track of your water system and on/off your plants water
    sprinkler.
  7. Go to Arduino to enter the Auth token, ssid, and password
  8. Place the esp32 board in the board circuit case, connect the two set of wires that are color coded red,
    white and green. Connect the red wire with the black wire that has a red cap to the water pump.
  9. Adjust pH meter to the right pH (6.5 for Cilantro).
  10. Make sure the cilantro plant is 4-5 cm long.
  11. Take them out of the cup and separate the roots.Then put them in the planting cup. You will notice that
    there are small holes at the bottom, put the root through the hole.
  12. Place half the planting cup with clay pebbles and the other half with rocks.
  13. Place the water pump inside, then put the thin part of the power adapter on the bottom of the circuit board
    case.
  14. Fill your bucket with 2.5 gallons of water and mix the hydroponics plant nutrient.
  15. Press ON in the blynk app and your water will go to the plants roots.
